1. There is no way I can learn all the articles to all the words. What are the rules?

A: First of all, you have to start believing in yourself. Second, here are some rules. Unfortunately, there are quite a few of them and many nouns don't fall under any of those categories.

2. Is this the right website for me to learn German from start to finish?

A: That's a dumb question so I'm glad you didn't ACTUALLY ask that. Absolutely not. As a matter of fact, I personally think that learning a language with any electrical device is a waste of time. However, I also think that repeating vocab using pieces of paper and a pen is a waste of ressources. So clicking on stuff here is supposed to be merely complementary to a REAL class (also let's not get used to online-classes, please. I understand that during COVID-19 there's no other choice. Call me old-fashioned but I don't like this webcam-business).

3. What's all this vocabulary? How do you decide what's A1, A2, and so on?

A: These nouns have been extracted from the "Motive" books. Some of them may repeat themselves or be reused as parts of other compound words, which I find to be a GOOD thing, since the goal here is to get a "feeling" for the correct article and not necessarily remember as many individual nouns as possible (which is also a good thing, though). But yeah, it's pretty arbitrary.

4. How many words are there in each level?

A: Over 600. The sequence is random and doesn't end.

5. How were you able to create such a magnificent website?

A: I watched html/css and JavaScript tutorials on Skillshare.com because the COVID-19 lockdown almost turned me into an alcoholic and so I had to start doing something with my time. Thanks for the compliment by the way, I really appreciate it.
